Your skin's surface is naturally acidic, sitting around pH 4.5 to 5.5. Chemists call this the "acid mantle," and it's not decoration — it's an active defense system.

## Why acidic matters

At that pH range, the enzymes responsible for shedding dead skin cells (proteases like kallikreins) work properly, your skin barrier lipids stay organized, and the bacteria that *should* live on your skin can outcompete the ones that shouldn't.

## What happens at pH 9

Traditional bar soap sits around pH 9–10. Wash with it daily and you temporarily strip the acid mantle, which:

- Disrupts the lipid organization that keeps water in and irritants out
- Slows down the enzymes that regulate healthy cell turnover
- Creates a more hospitable environment for less friendly bacteria

Your skin *can* buffer back to its normal pH within an hour or two, but doing this twice a day, every day, adds up to chronic barrier stress — which shows up as sensitivity, dryness, and a compromised barrier over time.

## The fix is simpler than you think

You don't need a pH meter. Just look for cleansers labeled "pH-balanced" or "low-pH," and if you can, avoid anything that leaves your skin feeling tight and squeaky right after rinsing — that tightness is your barrier telling you something just got stripped.
